Final Act — A Letter

my every lasting love. Click Reveal to read.

To her,

These seven months were real. They were messy and beautiful; they taught me how to love and how to lose. I have thought long and hard about what would be kindest for both of us. For my final act of love I will let you go not out of anger and not out of defeat, but because holding on to you meant losing myself.

The greatest form of love I can give you now is returning you to the person you love, the one I abandoned to keep you. This is not surrender; it is an act of care. I will remember the laughter, the small rituals, the music, and the kindness. I will take responsibility for what I could not keep, and I will learn from it.

Go back. Be whole. Find home in the arms that fit you best. I will honor you by honoring what is right for you even if that means stepping away.

With love,

(ken)

Made to hold memory.
